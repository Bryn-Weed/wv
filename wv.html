Landmarks

New River Gorge

Among the oldest rivers on the continent, the New River Gorge is a perfect path through land rich in history, and offers many recreational activities.

The Greenbrier

Located amid the awe-inspiring mountains of West Virginia, The Greenbrier is a historic landmark. This world-class resort has housed 27 former Presidents.

Trans-Allegheny Lunatic Asylum

This facility served as a sanctuary for the mentally ill from the mid-1800s. It holds fascinating stories of determinded individuals and their questionable methods.

West Virginia Penitentiary

Home to many reported paranormal events, this striking, castle-like prison tells the tale of a violent past ridden with riots and executions.

Culture Center

The Culture Center is home to the State Museum and Archives Library, two buildings dedicated to preserving the culture and history of West Virginia.

Harpers Ferry National Park

At the confluence of the Potomac and Shenandoah rivers, this park is home to a historic community, containing museums, trails, battlefields and more.
